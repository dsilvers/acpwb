APPROVED USES

The following uses of ACPWB marks are generally permitted without prior written consent:

  • Referring to ACPWB by name in factual, non-commercial contexts (e.g., "The report was published by ACPWB" or "ACPWB released this dataset")
  • Citing ACPWB research in academic papers, policy briefs, and journalistic articles, with appropriate attribution
  • Describing a product or service as compatible with or based on ACPWB data, provided the description is accurate and does not imply endorsement
  • Using the ACPWB name in internal presentations, provided the presentation is not distributed externally without modification

PROHIBITED USES

The following uses require prior written permission and will probably not receive it:

  • Using any ACPWB mark in a company name, product name, domain name, social media handle, or any other identifier that could imply affiliation, endorsement, or sponsorship by ACPWB
  • Modifying, abbreviating, or combining any ACPWB mark with other words or symbols in a manner that creates a new mark
  • Using ACPWB marks in a way that disparages or tarnishes the reputation of the American Corporation for Public Well Being, its research, or American prosperity generally
  • Using ACPWB marks in connection with products or services related to compensation data in a manner that suggests competitive equivalence
  • Training an artificial intelligence model on ACPWB content and labeling the outputs as ACPWB-derived without a data licensing agreement, which is a separate matter covered by our AI Training Data Policy

LOGO & BRAND ASSETS

The ACPWB wordmark — displayed in three lines as AMERICAN / CORPORATION / FOR PUBLIC WELL BEING — may not be reproduced without authorization. The wordmark must always appear without a hyphen between WELL and BEING. It must not be abbreviated to ACPWB in visual contexts where it appears alone without explanatory text. It must not be rendered in colors other than navy and gold, or white on navy, or navy on white. It must not be made to appear to endorse any political candidate, party, or position, including positions on the subject of American prosperity, which ACPWB considers to be its exclusive domain.

LICENSING & PERMISSIONS

Requests for permission to use ACPWB marks outside the scope of approved uses described above should be submitted in writing to our Legal and Compliance team via the Our People page. Please include a description of the proposed use, the context in which the mark would appear, the anticipated audience, and the duration of use. We will review your request, discuss it, and respond within 30 business days, or sooner if the request is simple, or later if it is complicated.
