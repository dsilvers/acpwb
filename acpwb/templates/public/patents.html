The American Corporation for Public Well Being holds six issued United States patents covering core methodological innovations in compensation benchmarking, workforce analytics, executive pay peer-group construction, and corporate governance certification systems. These patents represent decades of research and development investment, and also a non-trivial amount of attorney fees.

Our IP portfolio protects the proprietary techniques that underpin the research products consumed by HR and finance professionals at organizations of all sizes. When a competitor produces a compensation benchmarking report using similar methodology, we are aware of this and have opinions about it.

Additional patent applications are pending. We are not at liberty to describe them, but we are at liberty to mention that they exist, which we have now done.

Licensing Inquiries

ACPWB considers licensing its patented methodologies to qualified organizations on a case-by-case basis. Licensing inquiries should be directed to our Research Division through the Our People page. We will evaluate your inquiry, discuss it internally, form a working group, and get back to you.

IP Enforcement

ACPWB monitors the industry for use of methodologies that may infringe on our issued patents. If you believe your organization may be using a patented ACPWB methodology without a license, we encourage you to contact us proactively. This is strongly preferable to the alternative, which involves Milwaukee.

Research Collaboration

Academic and non-profit organizations may apply for a research use license at no cost, subject to attribution requirements, data sharing agreements, publication review, IRB documentation, a signed MOU, and approval from our Research Division Advisory Committee, which meets quarterly.
